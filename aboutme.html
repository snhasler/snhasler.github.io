About Me

Hi, I'm Sammy! Below I share a few things about myself. Feel free to read on to get to know me a bit better.

Research Interests

My primary research interests lie in reflected light direct imaging of exoplanets and Solar System science. My main PhD work focused on developing analysis tools and precursor science for future direct imaging missions (like the Roman Coronagraph and the Habitable Worlds Observatory).

My PhD dissertation focused on understanding how we can leverage orbital phase and color to detect and characterize planetary bodies from exoplanets to asteroids. I am interested in how an exoplanet's orbital phase and atmospheric composition can assist in "deconfusing" directly imaged multi-planet systems and inform our reflected light observations. I am also passionate about leveraging observations of our neighboring Solar System planets for exoplanet analog studies. You can read more about my research here.

I recently also started embroidering designs that correspond to each of the National Parks that my partner and I have visited together.
